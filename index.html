About

I am a PhD candidate in Ecology at Colorado State University studying conservation genomics and avian ecology. My research focuses on how populations adapt to environmental change and how genetic information can be used to inform conservation planning. I primarily work with alpine and migratory bird species, combining genomic data, environmental modeling, and spatial analyses to understand patterns of local adaptation, population structure, and vulnerability to climate change.

More broadly, I am interested in how evolutionary processes shape biodiversity across landscapes and how these insights can be applied to conservation decision-making. Alongside my research, I am passionate about avian biology more generally, as well as the intersection between science and art through nature journaling and scientific illustration.
